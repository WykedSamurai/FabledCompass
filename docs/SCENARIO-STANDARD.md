# Scenario Standard

## Purpose

Scenarios are structured workplace roleplays that assess observable decisions and provide constructive feedback.

## Required Scenario Fields

- Title
- Summary
- Industry or context
- Difficulty
- Estimated completion time
- Skills assessed
- Learning objectives
- Starting role
- Scene list
- Choices and consequences
- Scoring rules
- Automatic-fail rules
- Completion conditions
- Feedback templates
- Badge eligibility
- Version

## Scene Structure

Each scene must contain:

1. Context shown to the user.
2. NPC dialogue or workplace event.
3. Two to four meaningful responses.
4. Skill effects for each response.
5. Immediate consequence or transition.
6. Optional coaching note for the final result.

Choices should not rely on trick wording. A strong response may still involve tradeoffs, but users should be evaluated on workplace judgment rather than guessing what the author wants.

## Scoring

The MVP uses five skill scores from 0 to 4:

- 0: Harmful
- 1: Needs significant improvement
- 2: Developing
- 3: Effective
- 4: Strong

Scenario results convert the combined score to a percentage. The result screen must explain which decisions affected each skill.

## Feedback Standard

Final feedback must include:

- What the user handled well
- What could be improved
- Why key choices mattered
- One stronger alternative approach
- Badge outcome and criteria

Feedback must address behavior, not make claims about the user's personality or fitness for employment.

## First Scenario Definition

### Service Recovery Constellation

**User role:** Frontline customer service employee

**Situation:** A customer is upset about a purchase or service problem and demands an immediate solution.

**Skills assessed:**

- Communication
- Professionalism
- Empathy
- Conflict resolution
- Policy awareness

**Learning objectives:**

- De-escalate without accepting abuse
- Clarify the actual problem
- Explain policy accurately
- Offer solutions within authority
- Escalate appropriately when necessary

**Target length:** Five to seven scenes

**Possible outcomes:**

- Resolved within authority
- Appropriately escalated
- Customer remains dissatisfied but interaction stays professional
- Failed through harmful or prohibited conduct

**Badge:** Customer Service Foundations

## Quality Review Checklist

Before publication, confirm that:

- Every choice changes either score, consequence, or available information.
- No protected characteristic affects scoring.
- Policy expectations are stated or reasonably available.
- Automatic-fail rules involve clearly harmful conduct.
- The badge criteria match the documented scoring rules.
- The scenario can be completed without AI.
- All endings provide useful feedback.
