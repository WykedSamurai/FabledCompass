# Fabled Compass Decision Log

This document records major product, design, and architecture decisions so future work preserves the reasoning behind the platform.

## 2026-06-26 — Project Compass

**Decision:** Establish a unified dark slate application shell with a restrained cyan accent for the Career Journey.

**Reason:** Fabled Compass should feel like a calm professional workspace rather than a collection of disconnected pages.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Three Journey Accent System

**Decision:** Use one shared interface system with journey-specific accents.

- Career: Cyan
- Community: Amber
- Adventure: Crimson

**Reason:** Users should recognize context immediately without feeling that they have entered a different product.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Profile Renamed to Portfolio

**Decision:** Treat the user's professional identity as a Portfolio rather than only a Profile.

**Reason:** The system includes resumes, experience, projects, competencies, evidence, scenario reports, and downloadable materials. Portfolio better represents the scope and ownership of that work.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Users Own Their Portfolio

**Decision:** Users must be able to export the Portfolio they build through Fabled Compass.

**Reason:** The platform should not trap a user's professional work. Export formats should eventually include resume PDF, portfolio PDF, DOCX, and controlled share links.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Explainable Scores

**Decision:** Never show a score without explaining the reasons behind it and the actions that can improve it.

**Reason:** Portfolio Strength, Resume Strength, job match, scenario results, and readiness indicators should coach users rather than judge them.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Portfolio Strength

**Decision:** Use the term Portfolio Strength rather than Employability Score.

**Reason:** The platform can evaluate completeness, evidence, and preparation, but it should not claim to measure a person's worth or objective employability.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Assessments and Adventures Are Different

**Decision:** Personality and self-reflection tools are Assessments; evidence-producing workplace simulations are Adventures or Challenges.

**Reason:** Assessments help users discover preferences and should not be pass/fail. Adventures demonstrate competencies and can produce evidence.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Assessment Privacy

**Decision:** Personality and self-reflection assessment results are private by default and must not become employer hiring filters.

**Reason:** These tools support self-understanding and recommendations, not employment discrimination or performance prediction.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Communication Hub

**Decision:** Build one permanent Communication Hub rather than separate systems for AI, friends, recruiters, groups, notifications, and OOC chat.

**Reason:** A unified conversation engine reduces feature sprawl and preserves context across Career, Community, and Adventure experiences.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Communication as a Workspace Layer

**Decision:** The Communication Hub should open as a frosted transparent drawer over the active workspace.

**Reason:** Users should retain visual and mental context while talking to Compass, friends, recruiters, or roleplay OOC support.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Compass AI

**Decision:** The platform AI is called Compass and appears as a pinned conversation inside the Communication Hub.

**Reason:** Compass should function as a contextual coach, explainer, writing assistant, planner, and OOC guide rather than as a disconnected chatbot page.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Professional Compass

**Decision:** Demonstrated competencies should eventually be summarized through a Professional Compass visualization.

**Reason:** The compass provides an understandable, branded representation of direction and evidence-backed strengths.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Stars and Constellations

**Decision:** Scenario badges and related evidence can become stars that connect into competency constellations.

**Reason:** A professional sky creates a memorable visual record of growth that aligns with the Fabled Compass brand and avoids conventional trophy-based gamification.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Achievement Behavior

**Decision:** Progress should be acknowledged quietly rather than through confetti, loud banners, or intrusive animation.

**Reason:** Fabled Compass should reward curiosity and meaningful progress without interrupting focus.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Master Logo

**Decision:** The supplied celestial traveler-and-compass artwork is the master brand logo and visual reference.

**Reason:** It expresses navigation, possibility, stars, constellations, and personal journey in one image.

**Status:** Approved

## 2026-06-26 — Brand Asset System

**Decision:** The master logo should eventually be accompanied by a simplified compass mark and wordmark for small and interface uses.

**Reason:** The detailed master logo will not remain legible at favicon, sidebar, loading, and floating-button sizes.

**Status:** Approved
